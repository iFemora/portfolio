How I Approach Product Discovery

Published: 2025‑04‑15

Discovery is one of the most critical phases in product management. It’s where we define the problem, validate assumptions, and ensure we're building the right thing. Over dozens of discovery cycles, I've honed a repeatable approach that balances structure with flexibility.

Start with Questions

Instead of jumping to solutions, I lead with questions. Who experiences this problem? How often? What are the alternatives? Asking open‑ended questions helps uncover context and constraints that might otherwise be missed.

Mix Methods

Different methods reveal different insights. I combine qualitative interviews, quantitative data analysis, and quick experiments. For example, a cohort analysis may highlight churn signals while a survey explores the underlying reasons.

Align the Team

Discovery isn’t a solo sport. I bring designers, engineers, and stakeholders into the process early. Together we review findings, map journeys, and ideate. This shared understanding prevents handoff issues and builds ownership.

Decide & Define

Ultimately, discovery must lead to a decision: go, no go, or pivot. I synthesize insights into a clear problem statement, success criteria, and hypotheses. This becomes the north star for subsequent development.
