Payments Platform for Startups

Product Manager • 2021–2024

Context

In many African countries, startups struggle to collect payments from customers due to fragmented infrastructure and limited international support. Our goal was to build a platform that would enable local businesses to accept payments in multiple currencies, while complying with regional regulations and offering competitive fees.

Goals

  • Create a developer‑friendly API and dashboard to onboard merchants in less than 30 minutes.
  • Support payments in NGN, USD, EUR, and GBP from day one.
  • Achieve 99.95% uptime and process 500 transactions per second at peak.

Approach

  1. Validated the problem through interviews with 30 startup founders and payment processors.
  2. Partnered with banking providers to ensure compliance and liquidity management.
  3. Built a modular architecture with microservices and a React dashboard, enabling fast iteration.
  4. Launched a beta program, collected feedback, and iterated on onboarding flows and API design.

Outcomes

The platform went live in early 2024 and was adopted by over 150 startups within the first six months. Developers praised its ease of integration, and merchants reported faster cash flow. Transaction volume steadily grew, and we expanded partnerships with additional banks and card networks.

Metric Before After Change
Average onboarding time 5 days 30 minutes −90%
Peak TPS handled 50 500 ×10

Learnings

  • Investing early in a strong developer experience reduces support overhead and churn.
  • Regulatory compliance must be embedded into the product roadmap from day one.
  • Building relationships with banks and card networks unlocks competitive advantages.

What's Next

Following the initial launch, we plan to integrate advanced fraud detection, introduce recurring billing, and expand coverage to additional African and European markets. Continuous listening and rapid experimentation will guide future releases.
