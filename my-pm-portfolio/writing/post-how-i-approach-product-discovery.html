How I Approach Product Discovery

Frameworks and mindsets for understanding problems before building solutions

Published: May 14, 2025

Product discovery is about learning what to build and why. Over the years I've refined an approach that keeps me grounded in user problems while moving quickly.

Start with assumptions

Every project begins with assumptions about the user, the market and the value proposition. I capture these as hypotheses, which helps align the team and creates a testable plan. For example, we might assume that business owners struggle with reconciliation because their accounting and banking systems don't talk to each other.

Talk to real users

There's no substitute for qualitative insights. I conduct interviews with a diverse set of users, focusing on their context, motivations and existing workflows. Asking "why" repeatedly often surfaces the root cause of pain points.

Map journeys & opportunities

Visualising the user journey from start to finish helps identify friction and unmet needs. I use journey maps and service blueprints to make these insights tangible for the team. Opportunities are prioritised based on impact and feasibility.

Experiment & learn

Discovery culminates in experiments—prototypes, landing pages or concierge tests—that validate or invalidate our assumptions. Success isn't about being right; it's about learning quickly and cheaply.

By embracing discovery as an ongoing practice, we reduce risk and increase our chances of building something that truly matters.
