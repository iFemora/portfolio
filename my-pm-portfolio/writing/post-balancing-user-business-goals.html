Balancing User Needs & Business Goals

Making trade‑offs that serve both customers and the company

Published: April 2, 2025

One of the toughest parts of product management is reconciling what users want with what the business needs. Here are some techniques I use to navigate this tension:

Define success criteria together

At the outset of a project, gather stakeholders from marketing, sales, finance and engineering to agree on success criteria. This shared understanding acts as a north star when evaluating options.

Prioritise based on impact and effort

Use a simple 2×2 matrix plotting impact (for users and the business) against effort (design, engineering, operations). Features in the high‑impact/low‑effort quadrant should rise to the top.

Communicate trade‑offs transparently

When a decision favours one side, explain why and how you'll mitigate negative effects. For example, a feature that drives revenue but adds friction to the user flow might be accompanied by improved onboarding materials.

Balancing isn't about pleasing everyone all the time—it’s about making deliberate choices and learning from outcomes.
